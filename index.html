MY INSTAGRAM

The Wireframe:
I first start my projects with a single vertice then extrude it to shape it how I want, this time, I made a face

The Solid  viewport:
Then, I switched to the solid view to have better access to the overall shape and proportions of the face. This view helps me refine the structure without being distracted by textures or wireframes, focusing purely on the geometry and silhouette.

The Material Preview:
Next, I moved to the material preview to begin working on the textures and shading. This mode allows me to see how the colors, materials, and lighting interact on the model. I started by adding a base skin material and layering details like the eyes, eyebrows, and subtle blush to bring the character to life.

The Rendered Viewport:
Finally, I switched to the shaded/rendered viewport to check the final look of the character with all the materials, lighting, and shadows applied. This gives a more realistic preview of how the model will appear on the final render. I adjusted the lighting to ensure the face has the right amount of contrast and highlights, and fine-tuned the shading to make the features pop, ensuring everything looks smooth and polished.
